Lohagad Fort

Lohagad Fort, meaning "Iron Fort," is a historic fortress in Maharashtra, India, located near Lonavala and standing at 3,389 feet (1,033 meters) above sea level. Built in the 14th century, it served various dynasties and was captured by Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1648 before being surrendered to the Mughals. The fort is known for its architecture, beautiful views, and is a popular destination for trekking, especially during the monsoon season
